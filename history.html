The History of Seinfeld

Seinfeld, a sitcom created by Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David, ranked among the most popular television programs of the 1990s. The series ran for nine seasons and 180 episodes on NBC. Often celebrated as "the show about nothing," Seinfeld enraptured audiences with its explorations of the minutiae of everyday life. The series depicted the lives of a fictionalized version of Seinfeld, his best friend, George Costanza (played by Jason Alexander), his ex-girlfriend, Elaine (played by Julia Louis-Dreyfus), and his neighbor, Cosmo Kramer (played by Michael Richards).

In 2013, the Writers Guild of America voted Seinfeld the second best-written TV series of all time (behind The Sopranos).
